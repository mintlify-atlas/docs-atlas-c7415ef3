## What it does

`/opsx:onboard` walks you through a complete workflow cycle with narration, using your real codebase — not toy examples. It scans for a small, safe improvement opportunity, creates a change, generates artifacts, implements the work, and archives the result. Each step is explained as it happens.

## Syntax

```text
/opsx:onboard
```

No arguments. The AI guides the entire session.

## Phases

<Steps>
  <Step title="Welcome and codebase analysis">
    The AI introduces the workflow and scans your codebase to understand the project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finding an improvement opportunity">
    The AI identifies a few small, safe changes that would make good onboarding examples.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Creating a change">
    Runs `/opsx:new` to scaffold the change folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Writing the proposal">
    Creates a `proposal.md` describing the intent of the change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Creating specs">
    Generates delta specs capturing the requirements for the change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Writing the design">
    Produces a `design.md` with implementation approach and decisions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Creating tasks">
    Generates a `tasks.md` with the implementation checklist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Implementing tasks">
    Runs `/opsx:apply` to write the actual code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verifying implementation">
    Runs `/opsx:verify` to validate the implementation against artifacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archiving the change">
    Runs `/opsx:archive` to finalize and move the change to the archive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Summary and next steps">
    Explains what was demonstrated and points you toward the next steps for your own work.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example

```text
You: /opsx:onboard

AI:  Welcome to OpenSpec!

     I'll walk you through the complete workflow using your actual codebase.
     We'll find something small to improve, create a proper change for it,
     implement it, and archive it.

     Let me scan your codebase for opportunities...

     [Analyzes codebase]

     I found a few things we could work on:
     1. Add input validation to the contact form
     2. Improve error messages in the auth flow
     3. Add loading states to async buttons

     Which interests you? (or suggest something else)
```

## Tips

<Tip>
  Onboarding takes 15–30 minutes and creates a real change you can keep or discard. It's the best way to understand how all the commands fit together before using them on your own work.
</Tip>

- Best for new users learning the workflow
- Uses real code, not toy examples
- Creates a real change with actual artifacts and implementation
- The completed change can be kept as a reference or discarded
