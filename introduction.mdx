OpenSpec is a lightweight spec layer for AI-assisted development. It gives you and your AI coding assistant a structured way to agree on requirements, design, and tasks before implementation begins — so you get predictable results instead of vague prompts and unpredictable code.

## Why OpenSpec?

AI coding assistants are powerful but unpredictable when requirements live only in chat history. OpenSpec adds a spec layer that sits between your ideas and your code:

## How it works

OpenSpec introduces a simple workflow driven by slash commands in your AI assistant's chat interface:

```text
/opsx:propose add-dark-mode   →  Creates proposal, specs, design, tasks
/opsx:apply                   →  AI implements the tasks
/opsx:archive                 →  Merges specs, preserves history
```

Each "change" is a folder containing planning artifacts that guide both you and the AI:

```
openspec/changes/add-dark-mode/
├── proposal.md     # Why and what
├── specs/          # Delta requirements (ADDED/MODIFIED/REMOVED)
├── design.md       # How (technical approach)
└── tasks.md        # Implementation checklist
```

When you archive a change, its delta specs merge into your main `openspec/specs/` directory — building a living specification of your system over time.
